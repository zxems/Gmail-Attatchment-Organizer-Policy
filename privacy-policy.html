Privacy Policy for Gmail Attachment Organizer

Last Updated: March 26, 2025

1. Introduction

Gmail Attachment Organizer is a Chrome extension designed to scan your Gmail inbox, categorize attachments, and provide a searchable sidebar archive for easy access. This privacy policy explains how we handle your data to deliver this functionality while prioritizing your privacy.

2. Data We Collect

To provide its core features, Gmail Attachment Organizer collects the following data:

  • Email Metadata: Information such as sender, date, and subject lines to identify and categorize attachments.
  • Attachment Metadata: Details like file names, types, sizes, and custom tags, stored locally to build the searchable archive.
  • Limited Email Content: Portions of email bodies may be analyzed for AI-driven tagging (e.g., identifying an attachment as “Invoice”).
  • Authentication Information: OAuth tokens, managed securely by Chrome, to access the Gmail API.

3. How We Use Your Data

The data collected is used solely to enable the extension’s functionality:

  • Email and attachment metadata are processed to extract, categorize, and display attachments in a sidebar.
  • Limited email content analysis helps tag attachments for better organization.
  • OAuth tokens allow secure access to your Gmail account via the Gmail API.

All processing occurs locally on your device using Chrome’s APIs, and no data is transmitted to external servers.

4. Data Storage and Security

- Attachment metadata and the sidebar index are stored locally on your device using Chrome’s storage API to enable fast access and offline functionality.
- OAuth tokens are managed securely by Chrome and are not stored by the extension.
- We do not store full email content or attachments; these remain in your Gmail account unless you download them.
- No data is transmitted off your device, and we use Chrome’s secure APIs to protect your information.

5. Data Sharing

Gmail Attachment Organizer does not share your data with third parties. All data processing happens locally, and interactions with the Gmail API are handled securely via Google’s servers, not ours.

6. User Rights and Control

You have full control over your data:
- Uninstalling the extension will clear all locally stored data (e.g., attachment metadata).
- You can manage permissions (e.g., revoking Gmail access) via your Google account settings.
